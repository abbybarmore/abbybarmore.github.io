Is Big Ten football more competitive in the Scott Frost era?

2021-04-02

Is Big Ten football, in general, more competitive in the Scott Frost Era?

Since Nebraska joined the Big Ten in 2011, Bo Pelini is the only Husker head coach to win 10 or more games in a season and the only coach to win nine games in a season multiple times. When Pelini was fired after the 2014 season, ending his seven-season reign, Mike Riley was hired and went 19-19 in three seasons. NU brought in home-grown Husker, coach Scott Frost once it split ways with Riley in 2017. Frost’s team went 4-8 in his first season followed by a 5-7 record in 2019 and a 3-5 record in 2020.

The Big Ten football landscape has change tremendously since 2011. How competitive was the Big Ten when Pelini was winning games this century? Riley? Frost? Was the league less competitive in the early 2010s than it is now? Essentially, is it harder for Frost to win games in this Big Ten climate compared the conference of the coaches before him?

Let’s find out.
